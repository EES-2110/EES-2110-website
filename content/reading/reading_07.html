Reading Notes:

Convection

This chapter focuses on atmospheric convection, which is a fancy way of describing the fact that warm air rises and cool air sinks.

Figure 5-1 shows the role of convection in moving heat around the atmosphere. A layer model or purely radiative calculateion like we did with the MODTRAN model will predict a surface temperature that’s higher than the actual surface temperature. This is because these models ignore the way that warm air near the surface rises and moves heat from the surface to higher altitudes, while cool air from higher up sinks down to the surface. This cools the surface and warms the higher altitudes, and accounting for this produces much better predictions of the surface temperature and the greenhouse effect.

The chapter begins by describing the way temperature and air pressure change as you rise up from sea level to high altitudes. You should understand why both pressure and temperature change with altitude.

A key concept is the dry adiabat, which is introudced on p. 50.
